Red Velvet is a South Korean girl group formed by SM Entertainment in 2014. The group originally debuted with four members—Irene, Seulgi, Wendy, and Joy—on August 1, 2014, with the song Happiness. In March 2015, Yeri joined, completing the five-member lineup. Since then, they have become one of the most popular and successful K-pop girl groups. Red Velvet's music is known for its unique blend of two styles. Their "Red" side features bright, fun, and energetic songs like Red Flavor (2017) and Power Up (2018), which are full of vibrant energy. Their "Velvet" side showcases smooth, elegant, and R&B-inspired music, as heard in Automatic (2015) and Psycho (2019). This duality makes them stand out in the K-pop industry. Since their debut, Red Velvet has achieved numerous awards and hit songs. Their summer anthem Red Flavor became a massive success in 2017, topping charts in South Korea. Their song Psycho in 2019 further cemented their status as top-tier artists, gaining international recognition. They have also experimented with various genres, including pop, R&B, electronic, and even orchestral influences. In addition to group activities, Red Velvet members have explored solo careers. Irene and Seulgi debuted as a duo in 2020 with Monster, while Wendy and Joy have released solo albums. Members have also appeared in TV shows, acted in dramas, and participated in entertainment programs. Red Velvet continues to grow and evolve, bringing fresh concepts and new music to their loyal fanbase, known as ReVeluv. With their creativity, vocal talents, and stage presence, they remain one of the most iconic K-pop girl groups.
